Repair or Replace — FAQ
Answers specific to Mill Creek & Snohomish County
Most furnace repairs in Snohomish County run $150–$900 depending on the issue. Minor repairs like ignitors and sensors run $150–$400. Major repairs like blower motors run $400–$900. A diagnostic call typically costs $100–$175.
If your furnace is over 15 years old or repair costs exceed 30% of replacement value, replacement usually makes more financial sense. A 12-year-old furnace needing a $600 repair is a strong candidate for replacement — especially with PUD rebates available on heat pumps.

Simple tasks like replacing the filter are fine. Anything involving gas lines, electrical connections, or the heat exchanger requires a licensed technician — both for safety and to avoid voiding your warranty. Washington State requires permits for furnace replacement.
Most furnace replacements in Mill Creek are completed in one day — typically 4–8 hours. Jobs requiring new ductwork may take 2–3 days. Your technician will give you a realistic timeframe after the free estimate.
Gas furnaces heat faster and cost less to operate in very cold weather. Heat pumps are more efficient in Pacific Northwest winters, heat AND cool your home, and qualify for PUD rebates of $1,800–$2,500. For most Snohomish County homeowners replacing an aging system, a heat pump is worth serious consideration.
